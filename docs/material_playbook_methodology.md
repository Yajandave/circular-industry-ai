# Material Playbook Methodology

## Purpose

The material playbook layer prevents the agent from treating every stream as a generic waste-management problem. It adds material-family intelligence for circular economy intervention design.

## Method logic

The playbooks are structured around:

1. circularity question
2. high-value intervention patterns
3. prevention and design levers
4. value-retention levers
5. procurement and supplier-loop levers
6. industrial symbiosis partner types
7. evidence tests
8. red flags
9. routes to avoid
10. pilot patterns
11. KPIs
12. claim controls
13. ESRS E5 mapping
14. CTI-style metrics
15. fallback controls

## Design principles

- Prevention and value retention are considered before recycling.
- Hazardous, contaminated or legally uncertain streams stay under human review.
- Energy and water streams are not treated as ordinary waste-diversion opportunities.
- Circular claims are blocked until evidence supports the route.
- Playbooks are screening tools, not legal, safety, LCA or carbon verification.

## How this supports the LLM layer

The LLM should not invent material-specific circular economy knowledge from scratch. It receives structured playbook fields inside the resolution plan and uses them to write clearer reasoning, supplier questions and pilot guidance.

The LLM remains bounded by locked rules, evidence gates and claim boundaries.
